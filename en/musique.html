Title: The Hanging Tree

Artist: Jennifer Lawrence (performer for the Film)

Composer: James Newton Howard

Lyricists: Suzanne Collins (author of the “Hunger Games” trilogy), Jeremiah Fraites, Wesley Schultz

"The Hanging Tree" is a song from the "Hunger Games" universe, specifically present in the third book of the trilogy, "Mockingjay". It is sung by Katniss Everdeen, played by Jennifer Lawrence, in the movie "The Hunger Games: Mockingjay – Part 1".

Lyrics and Meaning

The song tells a tragic love story set near a tree where people were hanged, probably for treason against the Capitol. Katniss sings it for the first time when she is in the forest thinking about Peeta and other victims of the Capitol. With its dark lyrics and catchy melody, "The Hanging Tree" quickly became an anthem for the rebels fighting against the Capitol. It symbolizes hope, resistance, and solidarity among the oppressed.

Popularity

After the release of the Film, the song gained great popularity, partly due to Jennifer Lawrence's touching performance. It reached high positions in the charts of several countries and became a cultural phenomenon, often associated with protest and resistance movements.
